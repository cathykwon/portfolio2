“Floating or Sinking?”

As a contained body of water, swimming pools are more than just spaces that enable fun and leisure activity.
In preparation for our collaborated thesis, we designed a three part dictionary that includes terminologies related to our theme of sensory experiences: H– Hearing S– Sound V– Vision. This allowed us to further understand and abstract the textural components of the environment.

When immersed under water, our bodies are displaced both mentally and physically. Senses are magnified through a variety of altered perspectives; blurry vision, muffled hearing, restraint of breathing, etc. Our dictionary explores terminologies that examine the intimate interactions between auditory, proprioceptive, and spatial sensibilities such as light and water. Our subject addresses a larger issue on how we perceive objects through various kinesthetic systems and will provoke a journey that is not just sensory, but emotional and intellectual as well..
